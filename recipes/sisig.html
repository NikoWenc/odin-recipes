Pork Sisig

Description

Pork sisig is a popular Filipino dish. It can be considered as a main dish or an appetizer. The composition of this dish is mainly of pig’s parts such as minced pork meat, ears, and face. Chicken liver, onions, and chili peppers are also added.

Ingredients

  • 500g pork face (maskara)
  • 100g pork liver
  • 1 large onion
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 2–3 pieces calamansi
  • 2–3 Thai chili peppers
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper

Optional Ingredients

  • Mayonnaise
  • Knorr liquid seasoning
  • Egg
  • Butter or margarine

Steps for cooking

  1. Boil the pork
    • In a pot, boil pork face (or belly) with salt, peppercorns, bay leaves, and garlic.
    • Simmer for 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until tender.
    • Drain and let it cool.
  2. Grill or Fry the Pork
    • Grill or pan-fry the boiled pork until crispy and slightly charred.
    • Optionally, grill/fry the pork liver until just cooked through.
  3. Chop the Meat
    • Finely chop the grilled pork and liver into small cubes.
    • Aim for a mix of crispy and tender bits.
  4. Sauté Aromatics
    • In a pan with some oil or butter, sauté minced garlic (optional) and chopped onions until softened.
  5. Mix in the Meat
    • Add the chopped pork and liver to the pan.
    • Stir well and cook for 5–7 minutes.
  6. Season
    • Add soy sauce, calamansi juice (or lemon/lime), chopped chili, salt, and pepper.
    • Mix and cook until flavors are well blended.
    • Optional: Add 1–2 tbsp mayonnaise for creaminess (modern variation).
  7. Sizzling Plate (Optional but Recommended)
    • Heat a cast iron plate or sizzling plate, and brush with butter.
    • Transfer the sisig mixture onto the hot plate.
    • Optional: Crack a raw egg on top and stir in just before serving.
